Our Story

AuraSense was founded in Hong Kong to solve a problem that kept appearing in drone inspection deployments: the moment a drone flies beyond line of sight, into a tunnel, or under a bridge, cloud-dependent CV pipelines fail. Latency spikes. Connectivity drops. The mission stops.

We asked a different question. What if the drone's vision system worked the way a biological retina works — processing only change, not every pixel, at the edge, in real time?

SFSVC is the answer. A spike-based frequency spatial video codec that encodes drone video as sparse neural events, processes them through independent parallel lanes, and outputs structural defect detection in under 2 milliseconds — on a mini PC drawing 4.2 watts.

No cloud. No GPU. No compromise.

Principles

How we think.

01 — EDGE OR NOTHING

If it needs the cloud, it's not done yet.

Every feature we ship must work fully offline. BVLOS mission capability is a hard requirement, not a future roadmap item.

02 — BIOLOGY FIRST

The retina solved this 500 million years ago.

Neuromorphic computation is not a research curiosity. It is the correct architecture for low-power, high-speed visual processing. SFSVC is proof.

03 — ONE PROBLEM

We do structural defect detection. That's it.

We are not building a general-purpose drone platform. Deep focus on one problem is how you get to 99.2% accuracy at sub-2ms latency.

04 — HONEST BENCHMARKS

Real hardware. Real conditions.

All performance figures are measured on production hardware — Intel N100, Raspberry Pi 5, Jetson Orin — under real inspection conditions. Not lab synthetic benchmarks.
