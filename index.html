AuraSense SFSVC

Neuromorphic façade analysis for UAV inspections

Problem

Façade inspections in Hong Kong are under pressure from MBIS timelines, safety concerns, and rising building complexity. Rope access and binocular checks are slow, risky, and hard to repeat from cycle to cycle.

Drone missions generate thousands of façade images and point clouds, but inspectors still have to manually scan them for defects and judge which ones are truly "Imminently Dangerous" or "Require Repair". The result is noise, inconsistent decisions, and limited time left for engineering judgement on the areas that matter most.

SFSVC turns UAV data into structured, MBIS-aligned findings so Registered Inspectors, engineers, and asset owners can see façade risk clearly and act faster.

How it works

Step 1 - UAV capture

Your team flies a planned façade mission with a DJI Enterprise or compatible UAV, capturing high-resolution images and, where available, depth or LiDAR data for each elevation.

Step 2 - On-site ingest

After the flight, data is transferred to an AuraSense edge node - a compact Linux appliance deployed in the site office, control room, or plant room. No cloud upload is required.

Step 3 - Neuromorphic analysis

SFSVC converts video and images into spike-based representations and processes them in parallel, sub-millisecond lanes. Cracks and related façade features are detected and tracked across tiles and frames, preserving continuity along the building surface.

Step 4 - LiDAR geometry

When depth or LiDAR is present, SFSVC fuses geometric data with visual features to highlight bulging, delamination, and panel drift. This gives inspectors additional context beyond surface-visible cracks.

Step 5 - MBIS-ready outputs

The system produces colour-coded façade risk maps and structured, MBIS-style reports. Each finding is tagged with façade location, severity band, and wording aligned with Hong Kong inspection practice, ready for review, annotation, and filing by inspectors.

Technology

Neuromorphic spike encoding

SFSVC applies spike-based encoding to video and images, emphasising temporal contrast and changes instead of every raw pixel. This reduces data volume early in the pipeline and focuses computation on meaningful façade events.

On-device, low-latency processing

The engine runs on a small Linux node using SIMD-parallel lanes to keep per-frame processing in the sub-millisecond range. It is designed for mini PCs and embedded devices, avoiding dependency on GPUs or external cloud infrastructure.

LiDAR and depth fusion

Where depth or LiDAR is available, SFSVC fuses geometric features with visual information to detect bulging, surface roughness, and panel drift that may not be obvious from RGB imagery alone.

HK-calibrated risk bands

Risk bands, terminology, and outputs are calibrated for Hong Kong façades and MBIS workflows. The engine is tuned on local building typologies - from older tenements to podiums and marine-exposed structures - to produce findings in a form inspectors can immediately interpret.

Offline-first deployment

SFSVC is deployed as an offline-first engine, suitable for constrained or secure environments. Data stays at the site office or control room, under your control, with integration options to push only selected results into your existing systems.

Integration

Existing UAV workflows

SFSVC sits behind your current flight workflows. You keep your drones, your mission planning tools, and your pilots; the engine only changes what happens to the data after landing.

Reporting and documentation

Outputs can be exported into common formats or integrated with your existing document management and reporting tools, so inspectors and asset owners can maintain their usual processes while gaining additional structure and automation.
