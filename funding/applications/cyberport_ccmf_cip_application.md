## Section 4 — Innovation & Uniqueness

SFSVC is the world's only neuromorphic-native building facade inspection system. While every competing drone AI platform is built on convolutional neural networks requiring cloud GPU inference, SFSVC is architected from the ground up on spike-based temporal encoding — the same fundamental principle underlying biological vision. This is not an incremental improvement; it is a categorical architectural departure that delivers capabilities no competing product can replicate.

The core differentiator is the spike-based visual codec itself. Conventional CNNs process dense pixel grids at every frame, consuming enormous power and requiring retraining whenever building typology changes. SFSVC's neuromorphic codec processes only temporal differences — the sparse events that change between frames — consuming 10 to 50 times less energy per inference than GPU-dependent competitors while remaining fully operational on edge hardware without cloud connectivity. This means inspection data never leaves the building site, satisfying the most stringent client data sovereignty requirements.

A PCT patent application was filed in March 2026, representing the first patent filing in the specific application domain of neuromorphic spike-based building facade inspection. No existing patent landscape covers this intersection, and AuraSense intends to build a defensible IP moat around it.

Critically, SFSVC is the only system in the world that produces output natively aligned to Hong Kong's Mandatory Building Inspection Scheme risk classification bands — IA (Immediately Actionable), P1 (Priority 1), P2 (Priority 2), and ADV (Advisory). No competitor, domestic or international, generates MBIS-compliant structured output. STDP continuous learning enables the system to improve its defect classification accuracy autonomously with each new scan, without human retraining. ## Section 5 — Market Opportunity & Business Model

### Hong Kong Market

Hong Kong's Mandatory Building Inspection Scheme (MBIS), administered by the Buildings Department, requires all private buildings aged 30 years or above with 3 or more storeys to undergo periodic facade inspection every 10 years. With over 40,000 buildings in scope and a significant backlog created by the phased MBIS enforcement schedule, the annual addressable market for facade inspection services in Hong Kong is estimated at HK$2 billion to HK$4 billion per year. Current inspection costs range from HK$80,000 to HK$200,000 per building using traditional rope-access and scaffold-based methods, with turnaround times of 3 to 6 weeks per building. This creates structural demand for a faster, cheaper, and safer alternative.

SFSVC addresses this demand directly. At HK$8,000 to HK$15,000 per elevation scan and a 48-hour turnaround, SFSVC delivers a 60% or greater cost reduction and a 10 to 20 times faster turnaround versus the incumbent method. For Registered Inspectors (RIs) managing large building portfolios, SFSVC becomes an operational multiplier — enabling a single RI firm to process 5 to 10 times more buildings per year without proportional headcount growth.

### Greater Bay Area and Global Opportunity

Beyond Hong Kong, the Greater Bay Area presents an immediately adjacent expansion opportunity. Shenzhen, Guangzhou, and surrounding municipalities enforce increasingly stringent building inspection regimes aligned with mainland safety standards, and the GBA's high-density residential and commercial building stock creates a market comparable in scale to Hong Kong. AuraSense targets GBA market entry in Year 3. Globally, analogous regulatory frameworks exist in Singapore (BCSS), UK (Building Safety Act 2022), Australia (NCC), and across the EU, representing a total addressable market estimated at USD 12 billion annually for AI-assisted building inspection.

### Revenue Model

AuraSense operates a three-tier B2B revenue model:

**Tier 1 — Per-Scan Fee:** HK$8,000 to HK$15,000 per building elevation. Suitable for RI firms with irregular inspection volume. No upfront commitment required.

**Tier 2 — SaaS Subscription:** HK$3,000 to HK$8,000 per month per RI firm. Includes platform access, report generation, portfolio dashboard, and priority scan scheduling. Targeted at RI firms processing 5 or more buildings per month.

**Tier 3 — Enterprise Portfolio Contract:** Custom pricing for property management companies and institutional landlords managing 50 or more buildings. Includes dedicated edge hardware deployment, custom MBIS reporting templates, and API integration with existing property management systems.

The B2B RI firm licensing model is the preferred go-to-market strategy because RIs are the mandatory gatekeepers of the MBIS process — every building inspection report must be countersigned by a Registered Inspector. By integrating into the RI workflow as a productivity tool rather than competing with RIs, AuraSense avoids regulatory friction and creates a distribution channel that is self-reinforcing: each RI firm that adopts SFSVC becomes a recurring revenue source and a reference customer for the next.

## Section 12 — Social & Environmental Impact

SFSVC delivers significant social and environmental benefits that extend well beyond commercial value. On the public safety dimension, MBIS exists because facade failures — falling tiles, spalling concrete, detached cladding — cause serious injuries and fatalities to pedestrians on Hong Kong's streets every year. By enabling faster, more frequent, and more accurate facade inspections at dramatically lower cost, SFSVC directly reduces the risk of uninspected or under-inspected buildings contributing to such incidents. Inspector safety is equally important: traditional rope-access facade inspection is among the most hazardous occupations in Hong Kong's construction industry, with documented fatalities. SFSVC eliminates rope-access risk entirely by replacing human climbers with autonomous drones. Environmentally, the platform's ultra-low-power neuromorphic edge computing architecture consumes 10 to 50 times less energy than cloud GPU-dependent alternatives, and the elimination of scaffolding and rope-access rigging removes thousands of tonnes of temporary structure waste from the construction cycle annually. Data-driven maintenance scheduling enabled by SFSVC's continuous monitoring capability extends building lifespan by enabling early intervention before defects become structural failures.
